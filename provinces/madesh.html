Geography

Madhesh Province is located in the southern plains of Nepal, bordering India to the south. It is part of the Terai region and is known for its fertile plains, which are crucial for agriculture. The province covers an area of 9,661 kmÂ² and is characterized by flat terrain, hot climates, and rich biodiversity.

Districts

Madhesh Province is divided into 8 districts. Each district has its unique cultural and geographical significance:

  • Saptari: Known for its religious sites and agricultural productivity.
  • Siraha: Famous for its cultural heritage and traditional festivals.
  • Dhanusha: Home to the historic city of Janakpur and the Janaki Temple.
  • Mahottari: Known for its fertile land and religious significance.
  • Sarlahi: A major agricultural hub with diverse communities.
  • Bara: Known for its industrial areas and religious sites.
  • Parsa: Home to the Chitwan National Park and wildlife reserves.
  • Rautahat: Known for its cultural diversity and agricultural practices.

Rivers

Madhesh Province is rich in water resources, with several major rivers flowing through it:

  • Koshi River: One of the largest rivers in Nepal, flowing through the eastern part of the province.
  • Bagmati River: A sacred river that flows through the central part of the province.
  • Narayani River: A major river that forms the western boundary of the province.

Culture, Castes, and Religions

Madhesh Province is a melting pot of cultures, castes, and religions. The major ethnic groups include the Madhesi, Tharu, Brahmin, Chhetri, and Muslim communities. Each group has its unique traditions, festivals, and religious practices:

  • Madhesi: Known for their agricultural lifestyle and festivals like Chhath and Holi.
  • Tharu: Indigenous communities with rich cultural heritage, known for their traditional dances and festivals.
  • Brahmin and Chhetri: Follow Hindu traditions and celebrate festivals like Dashain and Tihar.
  • Muslim: Primarily located in urban areas, they celebrate festivals like Eid and Ramadan.
