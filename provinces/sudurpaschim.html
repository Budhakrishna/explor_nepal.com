Geography

Sudurpashchim Province is located in the far-western part of Nepal and covers an area of 19,539 km². It is bordered by India to the south and west, Karnali Province to the east, and Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north. The province is known for its diverse geography, including the Himalayan range in the north, the Mahabharat Range in the middle, and the Terai plains in the south. It is home to the Api and Saipal Himalayan ranges.

Districts

Sudurpashchim Province is divided into 9 districts, each with its unique cultural and geographical significance:

  • Baitadi: Known for its historical and religious sites.
  • Bajhang: A remote district with stunning landscapes.
  • Bajura: Known for its natural beauty and cultural heritage.
  • Dadeldhura: A district with historical significance and scenic beauty.
  • Darchula: Known for its proximity to the Himalayas and cultural diversity.
  • Doti: Famous for its historical and religious sites.
  • Kailali: A Terai district known for its agricultural productivity.
  • Kanchanpur: Home to the Shuklaphanta National Park.
  • Achham: Known for its cultural heritage and natural beauty.

Culture, Castes, and Religions

Sudurpashchim Province is a diverse region with a rich cultural heritage. The major ethnic groups include the Tharu, Brahmin, Chhetri, Magar, and Dalit communities. Each group has its unique traditions, festivals, and religious practices:

  • Tharu: Indigenous communities known for their traditional dances like Sakhiya and Jhumara.
  • Brahmin and Chhetri: Follow Hindu traditions and celebrate festivals like Dashain and Tihar.
  • Magar: Known for their cultural heritage and festivals like Maghe Sankranti.
  • Dalit: Known for their traditional crafts and music.
