Geography

Bagmati Province is located in the central part of Nepal and is the most populous province in the country. It covers an area of 20,300 kmÂ² and is bordered by Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north, Gandaki Province to the west, Province 1 to the east, and Madhesh Province to the south. The province is home to the capital city, Kathmandu, and is known for its diverse geography, including the Himalayan range, hills, and valleys.

Districts

Bagmati Province is divided into 13 districts, each with its unique cultural and geographical significance:

  • Kathmandu: The capital city and cultural hub of Nepal.
  • Lalitpur: Known for its ancient temples and rich Newari culture.
  • Bhaktapur: Famous for its well-preserved medieval architecture.
  • Kavrepalanchok: Known for its scenic beauty and religious sites.
  • Dhading: A district with diverse landscapes and cultural heritage.
  • Nuwakot: Known for its historical significance and fortresses.
  • Rasuwa: Gateway to the Langtang National Park.
  • Sindhupalchok: Known for its natural beauty and trekking routes.
  • Chitwan: Home to the Chitwan National Park.
  • Makwanpur: Known for its religious sites and natural beauty.
  • Ramechhap: A district with stunning landscapes and cultural diversity.
  • Dolakha: Known for its historical and religious significance.
  • Sindhuli: A district with a mix of hills and plains.

Culture, Castes, and Religions

Bagmati Province is a melting pot of cultures, castes, and religions. The major ethnic groups include the Newar, Brahmin, Chhetri, Tamang, and Sherpa communities. Each group has its unique traditions, festivals, and religious practices:

  • Newar: Known for their intricate art, architecture, and festivals like Indra Jatra and Bisket Jatra.
  • Brahmin and Chhetri: Follow Hindu traditions and celebrate festivals like Dashain and Tihar.
  • Tamang: Known for their Buddhist traditions and festivals like Losar.
  • Sherpa: Known for their mountaineering skills and Buddhist traditions.
