Geography & Landscape

Gandaki Province features dramatic geographical diversity from the high Himalayas to subtropical valleys:

  • Himalayan Region: Home to Annapurna (8,091m), Dhaulagiri (8,167m), and Machhapuchhre (6,993m)
  • Hilly Region: Middle hills with deep valleys including the Kali Gandaki gorge
  • Phewa Lake: Iconic freshwater lake in Pokhara with stunning mountain reflections

Cultural Diversity

Ethnic Groups

Gurung

Known for their bravery (Gurkha soldiers), rich culture, and traditional Ghantu dance.

Magar

Indigenous group with distinct language and cultural practices, famous for their Kaura dance.

Major Festivals

Tamu Lhosar

Gurung New Year celebration with traditional dances, feasts, and cultural programs.

Dashain

Nepal's biggest Hindu festival celebrated with special fervor in Gandaki region.

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

March-May and September-November offer the best weather for trekking and sightseeing.

Must-Try Foods

Gurung bread (Dhido), local honey, yak cheese, and Pokhara's fresh lake fish.

Top Activities

Trekking in Annapurna region, paragliding in Pokhara, and cultural tours in Upper Mustang.
