Geography

Karnali Province is located in the western part of Nepal and is the largest province in terms of area, covering 27,984 kmÂ². It is bordered by Tibet Autonomous Region of China to the north, Sudurpashchim Province to the west, and Lumbini Province to the east. The province is known for its rugged terrain, high mountains, and pristine natural beauty. It is home to the Shey Phoksundo National Park, Rara Lake, and the Karnali River.

Districts

Karnali Province is divided into 10 districts, each with its unique cultural and geographical significance:

  • Dolpa: Known for Shey Phoksundo Lake and its unique Tibetan culture.
  • Humla: A remote district with stunning landscapes and ancient trade routes.
  • Jumla: Famous for its apples and the historic Sinja Valley.
  • Kalikot: Known for its natural beauty and cultural heritage.
  • Mugu: Home to Rara Lake, the largest lake in Nepal.
  • Salyan: A district with a mix of hills and cultural diversity.
  • Surkhet: The provincial capital and a hub for trade and commerce.
  • Dailekh: Known for its historical sites and natural beauty.
  • Jajarkot: A district with stunning landscapes and cultural heritage.
  • Rukum (West): Known for its cultural diversity and natural beauty.

Culture, Castes, and Religions

Karnali Province is a diverse region with a rich cultural heritage. The major ethnic groups include the Thakuri, Chhetri, Magar, and Dalit communities. Each group has its unique traditions, festivals, and religious practices:

  • Thakuri and Chhetri: Follow Hindu traditions and celebrate festivals like Dashain and Tihar.
  • Magar: Known for their cultural heritage and festivals like Maghe Sankranti.
  • Dalit: Known for their traditional crafts and music.
